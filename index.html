About

From neuroscience toward robotics.

My academic direction has moved from neuroscience toward robotics. What keeps me engaged now is the combination of spatial reasoning, perception, action, and system building required to make embodied intelligence work in the real world.

  • I am especially interested in the bridge between multimodal understanding and physical interaction.
  • I care about the full execution loop: problem framing, reproduction, debugging, deployment constraints, and iteration.
  • I prefer work that connects model intelligence with concrete robotic capability instead of staying purely conceptual.

Research

Selected builds and reproduced systems

Recently I have been working around VLA spatial understanding, 3D reconstruction, and manipulation. These projects reflect how I learn: think through the system, rebuild it carefully, and solve the practical issues around hardware, compute, and physical setup.

Open project gallery

Vision-Language-Action

DreamVLA

DreamVLA integrates world-model imagination into robot manipulation policy learning through image and depth prediction.

3D Perception / Robot Manipulation

Tesseract

Tesseract uses stereo depth estimation and 3D scene reconstruction to strengthen robot policy learning with spatial representations.

3D Scene Understanding

VGGT

VGGT performs feed-forward 3D understanding, including camera poses, depth maps, and point-cloud reconstruction in a single forward pass.

Open-Source VLA

OpenVLA

OpenVLA is an open-source vision-language-action model for general-purpose robot manipulation, fine-tuned from a large vision-language model.
